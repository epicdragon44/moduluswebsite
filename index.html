Our Features

Intuitive and easy to use for everyone, students and teachers alike

Responsive

Our web app molds to fit the screen of the user, whether a large PC monitor or small tablet. That means anyone can access our courses, anywhere.


Centralized

Our platform allows teachers to centralize their courses from what would otherwise be disparate collections of resources spread across multiple websites, by organizing the content into a one-stop one-page shop.

Modular

Courses are comprised only of a list of modules, with each module comprised of items that a student can explore through. Each item is then marked as either Visual, Auditory, Reading/Writing, or Kinesthetic, based on the content.

Fast and Performant

Slow, laggy web apps that only work on Chrome, such as Canvas by Instructure, disadvantages the poor and underprivileged. Our web app is designed from the ground up to be fast and responsive on all devices.


Accessible

By integrating the VARK learning styles model into our application, users that prefer certain types of content over others can tailor their learning experience to meet their needs, and teachers are compelled to make courses that account for all the different kinds of learning preferences.

Fast to set up

Simply register an account, add a class based on its unique class name, and get to work. There's no installation of extensions, recurring authentication, or other bloatware.

Our Mission

In light of the recent COVID-19 crisis, we’ve seen staggering demand for online courses as students grapple with a reality in which education is now delivered over the internet. But traditional e-learning platforms like Khanacademy struggle to keep up with the pace of demand, while LMS platforms like Canvas, which requires teachers to sign up as part of large, wealthy organizations such as school districts, are difficult to use and lock out small independent teachers that just want to continue teaching. And on top of all that, all platforms rely solely on one medium of teaching, such as Udemy through videos, and Edmodo through text, without regard for user learning preferences. Modulus aims to fix all of that - in one cohesive online learning platform solution.
